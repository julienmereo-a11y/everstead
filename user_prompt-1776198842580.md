1. The trust layer — your biggest gap
Everstead asks people to store their most sensitive documents — wills, insurance, bank accounts. The thing stopping someone from paying £12/month isn't the price. It's "can I trust this company with my life's paperwork?"
What's missing:
SOC 2 Type II certification — expensive (~£20k) but mandatory for the Advisor tier
Privacy policy written by a real lawyer — not a template
"How we protect your data" page with real technical detail
Company registration — you need Everstead Ltd registered at Companies House
Without these, an estate lawyer will never recommend you to a client.
2. One killer acquisition channel
Right now you have no users beyond yourself. The entire £1M question lives or dies on this. You need one channel that works before building others.
My recommendation: the Advisor B2B channel first.
1 partnership with a London IFA or estate lawyer firm
They offer Everstead to their clients as a value-add
You get 50-200 families in one deal
Their endorsement solves the trust problem above
This is worth 6 months of your focus before anything else.
3. The emotional hook — marketing is missing entirely
The site is beautiful but it reads like a feature list. What converts in this space is story and emotion.
The homepage needs:
A real story — "My dad passed in 2019. We spent 4 months finding his accounts."
A clear villain — the chaos families face without a plan
Social proof from real families, not placeholder testimonials
You have the design. You're missing the narrative.
4. The delegate dashboard — the product isn't complete
Right now a trusted person can be invited but can't actually do anything. The delegate dashboard exists as a prototype but isn't wired up.
This is critical because:
It's the moment of truth for the product
An executor logging in after a death and finding everything — that's the wow moment
Without it, you're selling a promise you can't fully deliver yet
5. Recurring engagement — why would someone log in every month?
Estate planning is a "set and forget" category. People organise once and never return — which means they cancel.
What keeps people paying:
Readiness score that degrades — "Your plan was last updated 6 months ago"
Smart alerts — "Your life insurance policy expires in 30 days"
Annual review prompts — "A lot can change in a year. Review your plan."
Family activity — show when a trusted person viewed their access
The alert system is built in the database. It just needs the cron job wired up.
6. Legal clarity — what Everstead is NOT
You need a clear disclaimer built into the product and marketing:
"Everstead is not a legal service. We don't create wills or legal documents."
Without this you risk being confused with legal services — which attracts regulatory scrutiny you don't want at this stage
